How It Works Model Rules
01
FVG Detection
Scans the 9:31–9:59 ET window each RTH session for a classic 3-candle Fair Value Gap pattern. A bullish FVG forms when C3 low > C1 high, creating an unmitigated gap above. A bearish FVG forms when C3 high < C1 low. The gap boundaries define the trade zone.
9:31–9:59 ET Window
02
Entry & Stop
A limit order is placed at the FVG boundary on the retrace side — the near edge of the gap. The position is sized such that the stop is the C1 extreme (the wick that originated the gap), giving a defined monetary risk per trade. Risk is expressed in NQ points.
Limit at FVG Edge
03
Exit Rules
Position is split: 80% exits at TP1 — the POTQ (Point of Target Quarter) level at 10 bps from entry. The remaining 20% runner is held to end-of-day close (16:00 ET) or stopped out at the original stop level, whichever comes first. Combined R reflects both legs.
