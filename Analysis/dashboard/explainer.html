NQ Hourly Analysis — How the Studies Work

Two complementary studies on every NQ 1-hour candle since 2014, with diagrams.

One-sentence summary. Unlike Fractal Sweep and NPG (which detect specific entry setups and score trades), Hourly Analysis is a statistical microscope on every NQ hourly candle since 2014. Two questions: what happens after an hour breaks the prior hour's range, and what happens inside each hour split into four 15-minute quarters.

The two studies at a glance

Study 1
Breakout Follow-Through

For every hour that closed outside the prior hour's range (a breakout), the engine asks: did the next hour push further in the breakout direction (follow-through), or fade right back inside the bar (immediate reversal)? Then it conditions that probability on time of day, day of week, year, and where the breakout bar opened relative to the prior hour's midpoint.

Output: ~28,600 classifiable hourly breakouts over 12 years.

Study 2
Quarter-of-the-Hour Behavior

Every hour gets sliced into four 15-minute quarters: Q1 (:00–:14), Q2 (:15–:29), Q3 (:30–:44), Q4 (:45–:59). The engine then asks: where does the hourly high tend to form? The low? Does the high come before the low or after? How does Q1's direction predict Q2/Q3/Q4? How does Q1's range size predict full-hour range expansion?

Output: ~59,300 hourly candles with per-quarter OHLC, direction, range, and extreme-timing features.

Study 1 — Breakout Follow-Through (visualized)

Step 1
Define the breakout
A bullish breakout = the hour's close is above the prior hour's high. A bearish breakout = the hour's close is below the prior hour's low. Hours that close inside the prior range aren't classified — they're filtered out as no_prev or no breakout.
Step 2
What happens next?
The engine flags two outcomes on the bar after the breakout (call it H2):

Follow-through — H2 takes out the breakout bar's high (for bullish setups) or low (for bearish). The breakout extended.

Immediate reversal — H2 closes back inside the breakout bar's body. The breakout faded right away — a failed move.

Both can be true at once (H2 wicked through then closed back). The dashboard reports them separately.
Step 3
Conditioning the probability
The dashboard slices the follow-through rate by every combination of:

Year — is the edge stable? Hour of day (NY) — does the breakout hold during NY open vs late afternoon? Day of week — Monday breakouts different from Thursday? Direction — long vs short symmetry. Where H1 opened — above or below the prior hour's midpoint changes follow-through probability noticeably.

The Edge tab in the dashboard surfaces all of these as a yearly stability bar, a 24×5 hour-DoW heatmap, and ranked best/worst cells.

Study 2 — Quarter-of-the-Hour Behavior (visualized)

Step 4
Slice every hour into 4 quarters
Each 1-hour NQ candle is built from 60 one-minute bars. The engine slices those 60 bars into four 15-minute quarters — q1, q2, q3, q4 — and computes OHLC, direction, range, and the exact minute at which each quarter's high and low printed.

For the full hour, it records q_of_high (1–4) and q_of_low (1–4) — which quarter held the hour's extreme.
Step 5
Where extremes cluster
The empirical answer on 59,300 hours: Q1 and Q4 dominate. Roughly 36% of hourly highs form in Q1 (open-driven), ~29% in Q4 (close-driven). Q2 and Q3 — the middle of the hour — only hold about 16–19% each.

The same pattern holds for lows. Extremes cluster at hour boundaries, which is the foundation under "trade the macro window" intuition. The Quarters tab in the dashboard surfaces this directly.
Step 6
Conditional probabilities
Knowing Q1's direction shifts the probability of every subsequent quarter — by 3–5% in each cell. Small but persistent. Stack a few of these (Q1 direction + Q1 range size + hour of day) and the probabilities pull further away from 50/50.

The Conditional tab in the dashboard exposes the full Q1→Q2/Q3/Q4 cascade table, the conditional q-of-high table, hour-direction → high-or-low-first, and the Q1-range → hour-range expansion ratio.

How is this different from Fractal Sweep and NPG?

Same data (12 years of 1-min NQ), three very different intents.

AspectFractal Sweep / NPGHourly Analysis
Intent
What it produces Tradeable setups — specific entry/stop/target trades with win-rate, EV, profit factor. Statistical priors — probability tables you'd use to condition a setup, not trade directly.
Row unit One trade per qualifying setup. One hourly candle per row — every hour the market was open.
Output metrics
Headline number Win rate · EV · profit factor at 1R or 4-leg series-multi. Conditional probability rates — follow-through, reversal, quarter-of-extreme, etc.
Trade scoring Deterministic: each trade hits TP, SL, or expires. No trades — just classifications of what happened next.
Use case
How to use the output Open the dashboard, find a filter combo with edge, paper-trade it. Use the heatmaps and conditional tables to inform where and when to look for setups in the other models — or to build new ones.

The way to think about all three. Hourly Analysis is the foundation layer — the empirical priors. Fractal Sweep and NPG sit on top of those priors, asking "given the prior is meaningful, can we engineer a trade that exploits it?" The breakout follow-through rate, the Q1/Q4 extreme clustering, the Q1-direction cascade — these are the kind of facts that make a setup like the Fractal Sweep ":42 rule" plausible in the first place.

Glossary

Breakout
An hourly candle whose close is outside the prior hour's range. Bullish = closes above prior high; bearish = closes below prior low.
Follow-through
The next hour after a breakout takes out the breakout bar's high (bullish) or low (bearish). The directional move extended.
Immediate reversal
The next hour closes back inside the breakout bar's body. The breakout faded right away — a failed move.
Quarter (Q1–Q4)
A 15-minute segment of an hourly candle. Q1 = :00–:14, Q2 = :15–:29, Q3 = :30–:44, Q4 = :45–:59.
q_of_high / q_of_low
Which quarter (1–4) contained the hour's high or low. The headline finding: roughly 65% of highs land in Q1 or Q4.
extreme_first
H = high came chronologically before low. L = low came first. T = both formed in the same quarter (rare).
Conditional probability
P(outcome | condition). Example: P(hour closes up | Q1 closed up). The conditional rate vs the unconditional rate is the size of the predictive edge.
Hour-of-day (ET)
Eastern Time bucket the candle opened in. 0–23. RTH = 09:00–15:59 ET. NQ globex runs 18:00 ET Sunday → 17:00 ET Friday, with a daily maintenance hour 17:00–17:59 ET.
