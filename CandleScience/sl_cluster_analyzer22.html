What am I looking at?
Each bar represents a bucket of SL % values (grouped by 0.05% increments). The height shows how many of your grid combos passed PF≥1.00 at that SL level.

The gold bar is your dominant cluster — the SL % where the most strategies survived. Think of it as the market's natural "breathing room" at your fixed candle. If 300 combos cluster at 0.25%, that means price consistently needs at least 0.25% of room before going your way.

How to use it: The dominant cluster SL is your starting point. It's not the final answer — cross-check it against the MAE percentile table. If the cluster says 0.25% but your MAE 80th pct says 0.12%, you have extra breathing room built in and can potentially tighten.
MAE Distribution — Mode & Median
MAE Frequency Bar Chart
What am I looking at?
MAE = Maximum Adverse Excursion. On every trade that won, how far did price go against you before reversing and hitting TP? That distance, expressed as a % of your entry price, is the MAE.

MAE Median % — half your winners saw price move at least this much against you. This is your minimum viable SL to not get stopped out on 50% of your winners.
MAE Mode % — the single most common excursion value. The market's most frequent "dip before recovery" level.
The bar chart shows the full frequency distribution — the gold bar is the most common MAE bucket.

How to use it: Your SL should be at or above the 80th percentile MAE (see Percentile Table tab). Anything tighter and you're getting stopped out of winners that would have paid. Anything much wider and you're risking more than the market needs.
MFE Distribution — Mode & Median
MFE Frequency Bar Chart
What am I looking at?
MFE = Maximum Favorable Excursion. On every trade that lost or timed out, how far did price go your way before reversing? That distance as a % of entry price is the MFE.

MFE Median % — half your losers went at least this far in your favor before turning against you. This is money you left on the table.
MFE Mode % — the most common run-up on losing trades. The market's most frequent "tease" level.

How to use it: Your TP should be set below or at the 80th percentile MFE of losers. If your TP is higher than what your losers typically reach, you're setting it too far — you'd capture more trades by tightening it. The gold bar is the exact level where most of your losing trades turned around.
SL % vs Combined Edge — Scatter
What am I looking at?
Every dot is one SL/TP combo from your grid sweep. X axis = SL %, Y axis = Combined Edge. Brighter/larger dots = higher edge.

What to look for: Is there a clear vertical band where dots cluster high? That SL range consistently produces better edge regardless of TP. If dots are spread flat across all SL values, it means TP is driving your edge more than SL — your SL mostly just needs to be "wide enough."

Dots near the bottom = negative or near-zero edge — those combos fail. Dots glowing teal at the top = your elite combos. Hover over any dot to see its exact SL%, TP%, and Edge value.

Use the fullscreen button to expand and the sliders to zoom into specific SL ranges — especially useful if all your best combos are clustered in a tight zone.

🏆 TOP 10 COMBOS — Composite Score
How the scoring works
Step 1 — Hard Gates: Any combo that fails minimum trades, minimum PF, max drawdown, or max RoR is eliminated entirely. These are not debatable — a combo with 8 trades and PF 4.0 is statistically meaningless.

Step 2 — Normalize: Each surviving metric is scaled 0–1 across the full range of survivors. Nobody gets punished for being in a bad dataset — they compete against each other.

Step 3 — Weighted Score: Each metric contributes its weight × normalized value. SQN is weighted highest because it measures consistency — a system that makes the same amount reliably beats one that occasionally makes more but erratically. PF and EV$ measure profitability. DD is penalized — higher drawdown = lower score. MAE Buffer = SL% minus MAE median% — a positive buffer means your SL has breathing room above what the market actually needed.

The #1 combo is not necessarily the most profitable — it is the most consistently profitable with the best risk-adjusted quality. That is the one you study first.

How to use this
Fix TP → Find best SL: You know you want a 0.25% TP. Enter 0.25 and tolerance 0.02 — the tool finds every combo where TP is between 0.23% and 0.27%, then ranks them by composite score to find which SL produced the best results at that TP level.

Fix SL → Find best TP: Flip it — you know your natural SL from the MAE percentile table (e.g. 0.12%). Enter 0.12 and find which TP maximized performance at that SL.

Tolerance: ±0.02 means the pinned value ±0.02%. Tighten to ±0.005 for exact matches, widen to ±0.05 if you want more candidates.

The distribution bar chart shows how the free variable (SL or TP) is spread across your matching combos — useful for spotting if the best score is isolated or part of a cluster.
