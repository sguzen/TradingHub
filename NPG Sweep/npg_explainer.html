NPG Sweep — How the Model Works

A plain-English walkthrough of what the engine looks for, with diagrams.

One-sentence summary. NPG Sweep tests a published TradingView indicator's setup model against 12 years of NQ/ES data. Same data as Fractal Sweep, different rules for what counts as a valid setup and how to measure outcomes.

What is "NPG"?

NPG is the handle of the author who published the TradingView indicator "Sweep · CISD · MTF · FVG · Key Levels." It's a Pine v6 overlay indicator, MPL-2.0 licensed. This project is a statistical engine that reimplements that indicator's setup detection logic in Python so we can run it across 12 years of historical 1-minute NQ and ES futures bars.

It's a sibling project to Fractal Sweep — same database, same anchor-window semantics, same SMT logic. The differences are in what counts as a CISD and how outcomes are scored.

The four steps of a setup

Every NPG setup has the same shape, played out over 4 stages. Here's each one with a diagram.

Step 1
Wick Lick — the sweep
On the higher-timeframe chart (1H or 30M), the engine watches for a candle that wicks above the prior candle's high but closes back inside the prior range. In trader-speak: the market briefly took liquidity above the previous high, then rejected it.

The mirror image (wicks below prior low, closes back above) is a bullish Wick Lick.

Setups where the candle sweeps both the prior high and prior low and closes back inside are excluded — they're ambiguous (could go either way).
Step 2
CISD — the confirmation
CISD = Change in State of Delivery. The engine walks backward from the sweep candle on the lower timeframe (5M or 3M) and collects consecutive opposing-direction candles into a series. For a bearish setup, that's bullish candles. The series stops at the first opposite-direction candle, capped at 20 bars.

Then it tracks series_low (lowest body in the series). The CISD fires when a new candle closes below that series_low — that's the bears breaking the bullish run and confirming the reversal.

The whole process has to complete inside the same higher-timeframe candle's window.
Step 3
Projections — the four targets
Once CISD fires, the engine doesn't aim for "1R." Instead it sets four target levels, each measured as a multiple of the series range (the high minus low of the opposing series).

From the break_price (where CISD fired): 0.5×, 1.0×, 1.5×, 2.0× of the series range. Each level = 25% of position size. Stop loss = the original sweep extreme.

So if the series spanned 40 points, the four targets sit at 20 / 40 / 60 / 80 points beyond the break — and the position scales out a quarter at each one.
Step 4
Silver — the optional filter
NPG's signature filter, called Silver. Two parts, both must hold:

(a) Late-week timing. Either the 5th 4-hour bucket of the day (afternoon onward) or the 4th bucket on/after 1pm ET. The market is in its final session of the week.

(b) Aggressive close. The current candle closes beyond the extremes of the previous two candles in the trade direction — i.e., bears breaking two prior lows (or bulls breaking two prior highs). A decisive 2-bar break, not a fade.

Silver doesn't fire often, but when it does, the setup has earned both timing and momentum.

How is this different from Fractal Sweep?

Both engines watch for the same high-level pattern (HTF sweep → return → confirmation), use the same database, and live in the same hub. The differences sit in the details — what counts as confirmation, and how outcomes are scored.

AspectFractal SweepNPG Sweep
Setup detection
Sweep definition Sweep + return-to-range, no double-sweep exclusion explicit (handled by F4) Wick Lick: same shape, but explicitly excludes double-sweeps (candles that breach both prior high and prior low and close back inside)
CISD definition Single-bar engulf. The CISD level is the open of the earliest candle in a backward-running opposing run; fire when a single close breaks it. Series-based. Walks back collecting an opposing series (up to 20 bars), tracks the series_high/low, fires when one close breaks the series extreme. Sensitive to multi-bar consolidation.
Outcome scoring
Targets 1R (one full unit of risk = |entry − sweep extreme|). 100% exit at target. Four projection levels at 0.5×, 1.0×, 1.5×, 2.0× of the series range. 25% scale-out at each level.
Stop loss Sweep extreme. Sweep extreme. (Same.)
Composite R Always exactly +1R (win) or −1R (loss). Weighted sum across the four legs. A trade that hits TP1 + TP2 then stops out is partial win, partial loss.
Filters
Built-in filters F3 (Shallow Sweep ≤50%), F4 (Closed Back Inside), SMT (NQ-ES Divergence), and as of 2026-05-11: P42 (Late Extreme), PD (CISD in PD). SMT (same NQ-ES divergence). Silver (late-week timing + aggressive close). No F3/F4 analog.
Bookkeeping
Anchor window Sweep + return + CISD must all complete in the same HTF candle's time window. Same.
Same-bar TP/SL tie SL wins. SL wins. (Same.)
Data source candle_science.duckdb · nq_1m / es_1m Same DB, read-only.

The intuition. Fractal Sweep asks "did one candle close back through the sweep level?" NPG asks "did the entire opposing-side run get broken?" When markets consolidate before reversing, NPG waits for the full consolidation to break, which can mean different (sometimes better, sometimes worse) entries. And instead of a single 1R win/loss, NPG measures how far each trade actually walks, in fractions of the consolidation's own range.

Glossary

Wick Lick
NPG's name for the sweep pattern. A candle that wicks beyond a prior extreme and closes back inside the prior range. Double-sweeps (both extremes touched + close back inside) are excluded.
CISD
Change in State of Delivery. Confirmation that the sweep has actually reversed price direction. NPG's CISD is series-based — a whole consolidation must break, not just one candle.
Series
The run of consecutive opposing-direction candles walking backward from the sweep. Bullish series before a bearish setup, vice versa. Capped at 20 bars.
Series range
The span (high − low, body-based by default) of the opposing series. Used to size all four projection targets.
Break price
The series extreme that gets broken when CISD fires. For shorts: series_low. For longs: series_high. Projections measure from here, not from entry.
Anchor window
The time bucket of the higher-timeframe candle that hosts the sweep. Every part of the setup — sweep, return, CISD, fire — has to happen inside this window.
SMT
"Smart Money Tool" — divergence between NQ and ES. If NQ sweeps a level but ES doesn't sweep its corresponding level, that's an SMT — a structural disagreement that historically lifts win rate.
candleOfDay
A 4-hour bucket index: floor(hour_ET/4) + 1. Buckets 1–6 across the day. Silver fires only in bucket 5 (16:00 onward) or bucket 4 (12:00–15:59) when hour ≥ 13:00.
