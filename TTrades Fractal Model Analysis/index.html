Core Concepts

How TTFM° Works

What is a T-Spot?

A T-Spot (Trade Spot) is a supply/demand zone drawn when a new higher-timeframe (HTF) candle opens and C3 (the last closed HTF candle) has performed a qualifying sweep pattern. The zone spans from the close of C3 to the log midpoint of the triggering candle — defining a high-probability re-entry area.

Zone top = max(C3.c, sweep_mid)
Zone bottom = min(C3.c, sweep_mid)

HTF Auto-Selection

The indicator automatically maps the chart timeframe to its parent HTF. All T-Spot detection, CISD, and bias logic operate on HTF candle close events, not chart bars. The chart TF is only used for entry precision and confirmation.

T-Spot Variants

6 T-Spot Detection Patterns

All 6 variants share the same three-candle structure: C1 provides context, C2 is the reference candle whose high or low gets swept, and C3 is the trigger candle that determines the variant type. The zone activates at C3's close (the open of the next HTF candle). Only one T-spot fires per HTF candle.

Candle Roles

C1 — Context
Two candles back. Used only by Expansive variants to confirm C2 already made a new high or low.
C2 — Reference
The candle being swept. Its high (BEAR) or low (BULL) is the liquidity level that C3 must breach and return from.
C3 — Trigger
The variant-defining candle. Its sweep, close, and log midpoint determine which pattern fired and where the zone sits.
Normal Bearish SHORT ↓
C3.h > C2.h
C3.c < C2.h
C3.c < sweep_mid

The most common bearish setup. C3 spikes above C2's high — taking out buy-side stops — then closes back below C2's high. The close must also be below C3's log midpoint, confirming the wick dominated the candle. Conceptually: stops were run above the prior high, sellers absorbed the breakout, and price rejected hard.

Classic stop hunt High frequency
Normal Bullish LONG ↑
C3.l < C2.l
C3.c > C2.l
C3.c > sweep_mid

Mirror of Normal Bearish. C3 dips below C2's low — triggering sell-side stops — then closes back above C2's low with a close above the log midpoint. The lower wick represents a liquidity grab below prior support, with buyers stepping in to absorb the engineered selloff.

Liquidity grab High frequency
Expansive Bearish SHORT ↓
C2.h > C1.h
C3.c < max(C2.o, C2.c)
C3.c < sweep_mid

A two-bar expansion followed by a reversal. C2 must have already broken above C1's high (an expanding upswing), and then C3 closes back below C2's body top. Unlike Normal, C3 doesn't need to break C2's high — the expansion already happened in C2. C3 is the rejection candle.

Two-bar pattern Expanding range
Expansive Bullish LONG ↑
C2.l < C1.l
C3.c > min(C2.o, C2.c)
C3.c > sweep_mid

Mirror of Expansive Bearish. C2 made a new low relative to C1 (expanding downswing), and C3 then closes back above C2's body bottom. The pattern captures a downside expansion that fails — C2 drives lower and C3 recovers, suggesting the expansion was a fake-out absorbed by buyers.

Two-bar pattern Failed expansion
Pro-Trend Bearish SHORT ↓
C3.h > mid_level
C3.h < C2.o
C3.c < C2.l
C3.c < sweep_mid

The rarest and most aggressive bearish variant. C3's high only partially enters C2's range — reaching above C2's log midpoint but not reaching C2's open. Yet C3 closes below C2's entire low. The sweep was shallow but the close was devastating: a full range-extension breakdown signalling strong trend continuation downward.

Shallow sweep Aggressive close Rarest pattern
Pro-Trend Bullish LONG ↑
C3.l < mid_level
C3.l > C2.o
C3.c > C2.h
C3.c > sweep_mid

Mirror of Pro-Trend Bearish. C3 dips below C2's log midpoint but stays above C2's open (shallow pullback into range), then closes above C2's entire high. The wick into C2 was brief; the close through C2's high confirms buyers overwhelmed — a full range-extension breakout. Highest win rate of all 6 variants in backtesting.

CISD Candle Exclusion (all variants)

All 6 variants require that C3 is not a CISD candle. A CISD candle is an outside bar that closes back inside C2's range — it breaks both C2's high and low, then closes within C2.

(C3.h > C2.h AND C3.l < C2.l) AND (C3.c > C2.l AND C3.c < C2.h)

This prevents false T-spots during delivery sequences where price is actively being distributed or accumulated — the outside-bar-inside-close is a sign of indecision, not a clean directional sweep.

Zone Calculation

Log Midpoint Formula

The zone boundary uses a log-space weighted midpoint rather than a simple arithmetic average. The weight shifts toward the dominant wick, giving a bias-aware reference level that reflects institutional positioning.

Upper wick dominant
mid = ln(high) − upper_wick / 2
Result pushed toward lower half of candle
Lower wick dominant
mid = ln(low) + lower_wick / 2
Result pushed toward upper half of candle
Body dominant
mid = (ln(high) + ln(low)) / 2
Geometric mean — balanced midpoint
Final price level: sweep_mid = exp(mid)
Entry Confirmation

CISD — Change in State of Delivery

Detection Algorithm

①
Anchor bar (C2) identified as the last bar before the current scan window
②
Backward scan from C2: collect consecutive bars with opposite polarity to the expected direction (the "delivery series")
③
Series extreme computed: highest high (bearish series) or lowest low (bullish series)
④
Forward scan from C2: find the first bar whose close breaks through the series extreme
⑤
CISD line drawn at series extreme. Projections drawn at 0.5R, 1R, 1.5R, 2R of series_range
CISD_FAST_BARS parameter
Controls max lookback for the backward delivery series scan
Entry Trigger

Pivot Sweep Confirmation

Confirmation Sequence

Step 1 — T-Spot Touch
Price re-enters the T-Spot zone. Watcher activates.
Step 2 — Pivot Forms
ta.pivothigh(src, 2, 2) or ta.pivotlow(src, 2, 2) fires. Source = wicks by default, or bodies per usebody_for_confirmation.
Step 3 — Pivot Swept
A subsequent bar's high exceeds the pivot high (bearish) or low undercuts the pivot low (bullish).
Entry — Confirmation Close
Entry at close of the confirmation bar.
Stop = lowest low / highest high between pivot and confirmation bar.
Directional Context

HTF Bias Engine

GetHTFBias(C3, C2) — 7 Cases

Called at each new HTF candle open. Returns BULLISH, BEARISH, or NEUTRAL based on sweep direction and wick comparison.

1 C3 swept C2.h and closed above C2.h BULLISH
2 C3 swept C2.l and closed below C2.l BEARISH
3 C3 swept C2.h, closed inside C2. C2 upper wick > lower wick BEARISH
4 C3 swept C2.l, closed inside C2. C2 lower wick > upper wick BULLISH
5 C3 swept C2.h, closed inside C2. C2 lower wick > upper wick BULLISH
6 C3 swept C2.l, closed inside C2. C2 upper wick > lower wick BEARISH
7 No clear sweep or equal wicks NEUTRAL
Premium Setup

Silver T-Spot

★ Silver T-Spot Requirements

A T-Spot is upgraded to Silver when it meets all of the following criteria simultaneously. Silver T-Spots are flagged with a distinctive visual treatment to highlight the highest-conviction setups.

✦
Time Gate: candleOfDay == 5 OR candleOfDay == 4 AND NY_hour ≥ 13 — only forms on the 5th HTF candle of the day (or 4th if afternoon session)
✦
Multi-level break (bearish): C3.close breaks below all of C2.low, C1.low, C4.low — price punches through 3 consecutive prior candle lows
✦
Multi-level break (bullish): C3.close breaks above all of C2.high, C1.high, C4.high — price punches through 3 consecutive prior candle highs
✦
Standard T-Spot valid: any of the 6 variants must independently qualify (Normal / Expansive / Pro-trend, Bull or Bear)
Rationale: The 5th candle of the day aligns with the highest-volume institutional window. Breaking through 3 consecutive prior HTF extremes indicates a significant liquidity sweep, maximising the probability of a strong reversal from the T-Spot zone.

Position Sizing

The indicator calculates and displays a position size label at entry given account size, risk %, entry price, and stop price: 

risk_dollars      = account_size × risk_pct
risk_per_contract = abs(entry − stop) × tick_value / tick_size
contracts         = floor(risk_dollars / risk_per_contract)

// NQ tick: $5 / 0.25pt  |  ES tick: $12.50 / 0.25pt
